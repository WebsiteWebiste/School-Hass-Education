What is Humanities and Social Sciences (HASS)?

What is HASS?

Humanities and Social Sciences (HASS) integrates the study of history, geography, economics, and civics to help students understand the world around them and their place in it. It encourages students to explore key themes like social justice, sustainability, and the impact of historical events on society.

Why We Teach Humanities

Rationale for Teaching HASS

HASS provides students with an essential understanding of the world and equips them with critical thinking, problem-solving, and ethical reasoning skills. Through exploring the complexities of society, students develop an awareness of their own role in the community and beyond.

Key Skills from Humanities Education

Qualities and Skills

  • Critical Thinking: Analyzing and evaluating information to form reasoned conclusions.
  • Problem-Solving: Identifying solutions to complex societal issues.
  • Compassion: Understanding and respecting diverse perspectives and cultures.
  • Communication: Effectively presenting and discussing ideas, both verbally and in writing.

Respect, Relationships & Reconciliation (3Rs)

Our 3Rs Project

The 3Rs Project focuses on building respectful relationships between students, teachers, and the broader community. Through activities that highlight Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander histories and cultures, our students engage in meaningful conversations about social justice, cultural understanding, and reconciliation.

Indigenous Perspectives in Education

Indigenous Perspectives

We incorporate Indigenous perspectives throughout our HASS curriculum to provide students with a deeper understanding of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander cultures, histories, and languages. This approach aligns with the Cross-curriculum Priorities and AITSL Standards, fostering respect and reconciliation among students and the broader community.

Place-Based Learning and Excursions

Place-Based Learning

Place-based learning connects students to their local community and environment. We regularly use cultural centres, like Bunjilaka, to explore Indigenous artefacts and histories. Students also visit significant local sites, such as historical landmarks and cultural institutions, to deepen their understanding of the world around them.

Our School Celebrations

Celebrating Achievements

We celebrate student achievements through weekly recognition ceremonies, special events for significant Australian dates, and end-of-term showcases where students present their learning projects. This helps foster a sense of pride and accomplishment within our school community.

Our Teaching Pedagogy

Our Teaching Approach

Our teaching team employs a diverse range of pedagogical approaches to ensure comprehensive learning experiences:

  • Inquiry-Based Learning: We encourage students to ask questions, investigate, and discover knowledge through hands-on experiences.
  • Multiple Intelligences: Following Gardner's theory, we design activities that cater to different learning styles and strengths.
  • Narrative Pedagogy: We use storytelling to make complex concepts more accessible and engaging.
  • Place-Based Learning: We connect learning to local environments and communities to make education more relevant and meaningful.

For more detailed information about our pedagogical approaches, visit QUT's educational resources.
